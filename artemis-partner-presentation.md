# Artemis — Partner Presentation
## The 1st Analytics & Automation Platform for Philippine COD E-commerce

---

## The Problem

### Philippine COD e-commerce is massive — but operationally broken.

- Over 60% of PH online purchases are still COD
- Average RTS (Return-to-Sender) rate: **20-40%** across COD sellers
- Most sellers **don't know their real RTS rate** — they guess
- Every failed parcel costs ₱300-500+ when you add up all hidden costs
- A mid-sized seller (3,000 orders/mo) bleeds **₱387,000/month** to RTS at 30%

### Nobody is solving this.

There is no analytics platform built specifically for Philippine COD e-commerce. Sellers use spreadsheets, manual counting, or nothing at all. They scale ads to drive volume while 30% of their orders quietly fail.

---

## The Solution: Artemis

### What it does

Artemis connects to **Pancake POS** — the most widely used COD order management system in the Philippines — and provides:

1. **Sales Analytics** — Revenue, orders, AOV, trends across all pages
2. **Delivery Analytics** — Delivery success rates, attempt counts, courier performance
3. **RTS Analytics** — Return rates by page, product, city, time period
4. **Parcel Journey** — Per-order tracking with automatic customer notifications via Chat or SMS
5. **Operational Insights** — Fulfillment lead times, bottleneck identification
6. **Multi-workspace with role-based access** — Teams with granular permissions

### How it works

1. Seller signs up (2 minutes)
2. Connects their Pancake POS page(s)
3. Artemis pulls order, delivery, and customer data automatically
4. Seller sees their real numbers — RTS rate, bleed, problem areas
5. Turns on Parcel Journey notifications → RTS starts dropping

## Possible Questions from Partners

### About the product

**Q: Why Pancake POS only? What about Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop?**
A: Pancake is where the majority of Philippine COD sellers operate — it's the dominant POS for Facebook-driven COD. We start where the biggest pain is. Marketplace integrations (Shopee, Lazada, TikTok Shop) are on the roadmap but not launch priority — those platforms already have some built-in analytics.

**Q: What if a seller already tracks RTS in spreadsheets?**
A: Spreadsheets are always behind (3-5 days), can't break down by page/city/product in real-time, and can't automate notifications. Artemis replaces the manual work and adds automation on top. Most spreadsheet users convert fastest because they already value data — they just want better data.

**Q: How accurate is the data?**
A: We pull directly from Pancake POS via API. The data is as accurate as what's in Pancake. We don't modify or estimate — we calculate from actual order and delivery records.

**Q: What's the Parcel Journey notification? What does the customer receive?**
A: Automated messages at key delivery milestones — order confirmed, shipped, out for delivery, delivery attempt failed. Sent via Messenger (Chat) or SMS. The customer knows when to expect the parcel, reducing "not home" RTS by 4-8 percentage points.

---

### About the pricing

**Q: Why charge per delivered order for Parcel Journey instead of a flat fee?**
A: Alignment. We only make money on Parcel Journey when the seller's delivery succeeds. If a parcel is RTS, we don't charge. This means our incentive is the same as the seller's — more successful deliveries = more revenue for both.

**Q: ₱1 per 5 delivered orders — is that expensive?**
A: That's ₱0.20 per delivered order. For a seller with ₱500 AOV and 35% margin, each delivered order earns ₱175 profit. Paying ₱0.20 to help ensure that delivery happens is a rounding error — 0.11% of the profit per order.

**Q: What if a seller doesn't use Parcel Journey?**
A: Base plan still includes full analytics — sales, delivery, RTS dashboards. Parcel Journey is optional. But we expect ~50% adoption because the ROI is immediate and obvious.

**Q: Why not charge per order instead of per delivered order?**
A: Because charging on RTS orders would feel punitive — "you're charging me for my failures." Charging on delivered orders feels like a success fee. Psychology matters.

**Q: What happens when a seller exceeds their order cap?**
A: They're prompted to upgrade. We don't cut them off mid-month — orders still process. But they'll need to upgrade before the next billing cycle if they consistently exceed.

**Q: Why no annual discount yet?**
A: We're launching. Annual discounts (2 months free) will come once we've validated retention and have enough data on churn. Offering annual too early locks in revenue but masks churn problems.

---

### About the market

**Q: How big is the addressable market?**
A: Conservative estimate: 5,000-10,000 active Pancake POS sellers in the Philippines doing COD at meaningful volume (500+ orders/mo). At ₱2,244 ARPU, even capturing 5% = 250-500 customers = ₱6.7M-₱13.4M ARR. The market grows as more sellers adopt COD and Pancake.

**Q: Who are the competitors?**
A: No direct competitor in the PH COD analytics space. Indirect alternatives: spreadsheets (manual, slow), Pancake's own basic dashboard (limited analytics, no notifications), generic BI tools (not built for COD). Artemis is purpose-built for this niche.

**Q: What's the moat?**
A: Three things: (1) Pancake POS integration depth — we're first to build deep analytics on top of Pancake data; (2) COD-specific features like Parcel Journey and RTS analytics that generic tools don't have; (3) Network effects from aggregated data — the more sellers we onboard, the better our benchmarks and recommendations become.

**Q: What's the churn risk?**
A: Main risk is sellers who "see the number" and think they can fix it themselves without ongoing analytics. Mitigation: Parcel Journey notifications create ongoing dependency (sellers won't turn off something that's actively reducing RTS), and deeper analytics (city heatmaps, trend tracking) provide continuous value beyond the initial insight.

---

### About revenue and growth

**Q: When does the business break even?**
A: ~30 paying customers (~₱73k MRR) for a lean 1-person operation. This covers infrastructure, SMS costs, and a modest salary. Achievable by Month 2-3 with focused launch.

**Q: What's realistic Year 1 revenue?**
A: Conservative ₱1.9M, moderate ₱3.8M, optimistic ₱6.2M. The moderate scenario assumes 250 paying customers by Month 12 at ₱2,244 ARPU.

**Q: What's the CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)?**
A: Early stage: near zero (organic — COD seller Facebook groups, word of mouth, content marketing via blog and RTS calculator tool). As we scale: estimated ₱3,000-5,000 CAC through targeted Facebook ads to Pancake sellers. With ₱2,244 ARPU, payback period is under 2 months.

**Q: What's the LTV?**
A: At 5% monthly churn (mature), average customer lifetime = 20 months. LTV = 20 × ₱2,244 = **₱44,880**. LTV:CAC ratio of 9-15x — healthy SaaS economics.
